What is the purpose of the target attribute in links?

The target attribute is used to control where the linked page will open. For example, "_blank" opens the link in a new tab, while "_self" opens it in the same tab.

Why are relative links commonly used in websites?

Relative links are commonly used because they make websites easier to manage. They work even when files are moved to another folder or uploaded to a server without changing the links.

Where are links commonly used on real websites?

Links are commonly used in navigation menus, buttons, social media icons, product pages, blog posts, and contact pages. For example, "Home", "About", and "Contact" buttons are all links.
